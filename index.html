Government Policies for Rainwater Harvesting

Atal Bhuj Yojana:

It is a Central Sector Scheme with an outlay of Rs. 6,000 crores in identified water-stressed areas of 8,220 Gram Panchayats (GPs) under 229 blocks in 80 districts of 7 States viz. Gujarat, Haryana, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, and Uttar Pradesh to arrest the decline in groundwater level through community-led sustainable groundwater management.

Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Scheme:

includes water conservation and water harvesting structures as one of the activities under its natural resource management (NRM) component. Mission Amrit Sarovar was launched on National Panchayati Raj Day on 24th April 2022 as a part of the celebration of Azadi ka Amrit Mahotsav to conserve water for the future.
The Mission is aimed at developing and rejuvenating 75 water bodies in each district of the country

“Jal Shakti Abhiyan: Catch the Rain” (JSA: CTR):

In 2021 with the theme “Catch the Rain Where it Falls When it Falls” to cover all the blocks of all districts (rural as well as urban areas) across the country. focuses on the development of basic urban infrastructure especially water supply & access to tap connection to every household in 500 cities.

Mission Amrit Sarovar:

was launched on National Panchayati Raj Day on 24th April 2022 as a part of the celebration of Azadi ka Amrit Mahotsav to conserve water for the future. The Mission is aimed at developing and rejuvenating 75 water bodies in each district of the country

Surface Minor Irrigation (SMI) and Repair, Renovation & Restoration (RRR) of Water Bodies:

have multiple objectives expanding the cultivable area under assured irrigation by improvement and restoration of water bodies inter alia increasing groundwater recharge and revival of lost irrigation potential.

Way Forward

To revitalize India's dwindling water reserves, we must combine ancient techniques like percolation tanks, injection wells, and subsurface barriers with modern methods to harvest rainwater. This synergy is crucial to address India's ongoing water crisis.

  • Rejuvenate and replenish built-up and natural water bodies
  • Increase repurposing of cleaned wastewater by neighborhood sewage treatment facilities
  • Install properly constructed rainwater collection systems in residential and commercial areas
  • Collaborate between federal and state governments to enable these initiatives
  • Implement strict policy actions for long-term and sustainable solutions
  • Local government entities must propose and execute an integrated water management strategy
